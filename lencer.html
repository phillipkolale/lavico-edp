EDUCATIONAL BACKGROUND :  
2021:   Doctor of Philosophy in Literature, University of Nairobi, Kenya   Thesis Title: “The Aesthetics of Crime in Kenyan Films: An Examination of the Paradox of Painful Art in Nairobi Half Life and The Kitchen Toto. The thesisinterrogates two films; Nairobi half Life and The Kitchen Toto, with a view to establish why people would watch a film full of criminal activities, and which elicits pain when they avoid such situations in real life. The research intends to explain why although crime films contain objects of distress, audiences appear to seek them out and many artists attempt to create artworks that are painful.   
 
2013:   Masters of Arts in Literature, University of Nairobi, Kenya   Thesis Title: Reconstruction of Fantasy in Digitalized Folktales: A case study of   Tinga Tinga Tales” The thesis interrogates the role of Digital technology as a medium for repackaging Africa’s folktales in form of films with a view to establish the impact of the digital technology on the African oral genres.
