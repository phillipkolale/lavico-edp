BIO-DATA:
Dr. Ochieng is a social scientist experienced in baseline surveys and empirical research interest in environmental management, water demand and utilization, water resources, rainfall and drought trends analysis, food security and pastoral resilience.  He has worked with researchers from Kenya, United Kingdom and Mongolia. He recently participated in a project on Pastoral Resilience funded Global Challenges Research Fund and is currently a consultant with International Livestock Research Institute (ILRI).

This was a foundation building call, designed to bring together new teams of researchers to explore innovative approaches to resilience, to develop and test new questions and to better understand policy uptake and ‘pathways to impact’, with a view to shaping larger scale research projects in the future  
Research Project 3:·

Funding - Ksh 300,000 ·         
Duration: 2 years (2016 – 2017) 
Title: Use of Seasonal Climate Forecast and Drought Effects on Livestock Assets in Baringo County, Kenya The project was co-funded by Egerton University. The study assessed trends and severity of drought events, their effects on livestock assets, effectiveness and usefulness of Seasonal Climate Forecast (SCF) in predicting drought events, and identified enabling conditions for better use of SCF among the pastoral communities.
